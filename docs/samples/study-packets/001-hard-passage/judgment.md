# Judgment

## Before

I leaned strongly toward an individual-election reading before studying the
chapter's flow.

## Conclusion

I now hold that Romans 9 is primarily arguing about the faithfulness of God's word
to the covenant, and that the mercy language serves that argument rather than
standing alone as an abstract decree. I hold this with medium confidence.

## Changed view

I moved from a confident individual-only reading to holding both emphases in
tension, weighted toward the corporate-covenant frame.
