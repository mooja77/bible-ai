## Synthesis (confidence: medium)

Romans 9 is read across a spectrum. A corporate-election reading emphasizes
covenant history and the identity of Israel; an individual-election reading
emphasizes God's freedom in mercy. The chapter sustains serious tension between
these, and the strongest treatments hold the mercy language and the corporate
context together rather than collapsing one into the other.

_Confidence rationale:_ The positions have real textual support and direct
mutual challenge, so a single confident verdict would overstate the evidence.

## Positions

### Corporate election emphasis - 52%

Romans 9 can be read as emphasizing covenant history and corporate identity
before individual salvation mechanics.

_Why not higher:_ It must still account for the individual-sounding mercy language
in 9:15-16.

### Individual election emphasis - 48%

Romans 9 can be read as teaching God's freedom in individual election and mercy.

_Why not higher:_ It must still account for the covenant-history framing of 9:6.

## Unresolved tensions

- Whether "Israel" in 9:6 is primarily corporate or representative.
- How 9:16's "not of him that willeth" bears on human responsibility.
